How I'm Paying Off $47K in Car Debt at 18% APR — A Real Payoff Plan

I bought a 2020 Dodge Charger Scat Pack in January 2023 because the V8 rumble made my lizard brain happy. Five minutes after signing, I realized $47,312 at 18.24 % APR for 72 months was a financial choke-hold. That’s about $7,700 in interest the first year alone if I only paid the minimum of $1,088.

I’m not letting a car steal six figures from my future. Here’s the exact plan I’m running right now to kill the loan by July 2026 instead of January 2029, saving $18,430 in interest.

2. Extra Payment Formula I Use#

I send two extra chunks every month:

  1. Automatic $400 principal-only payment from every bi-weekly W-2 paycheck (=$800/mo).
  2. $1,200–$1,500 from DoorDash + weekend detailing gigs, paid the day the money hits my account.

That’s $2,000–$2,300 extra per month on top of the $1,088 minimum.
Every extra cent hits principal only — I triple-check the payment memo to keep the lender from “helpfully” prepaying future interest.

3. How Much Interest I’m Torching#

I keep a separate checking account nicknamed “Kill the Charger.” Every gig dollar lands there and is auto-swept to the loan within 24 hrs. Out of sight, out of mind, out of debt.

6. The Rules I Follow So I Don’t Quit#

  • No lifestyle creep. I meal-prep rice + chicken and brew cheap coffee. The car already ate my budget.
  • Weekly balance text. I set an IFTTT applet that texts me the loan balance every Monday. Watching it shrink is the dopamine hit.
  • One guilt-free spend. $50/month goes into a “fun” envelope. Keeps me sane without nuking the plan.

7. What Happens After#

Once the title is in my hand:

  1. Redirect the entire $3,238/mo to max out Roth IRA + HSA.
  2. Bump emergency fund from $3k to $10k.
  3. Sell the Charger, buy a $10k Corolla in cash. (Yeah, I’ll miss the V8, but 35 mpg and no payment tastes better.)

TL;DR#

I’m throwing every extra dollar at a $47k, 18 % car loan, cutting the payoff from 55 months to 42 and saving $18,430 in interest. Side hustles + automation + zero mercy is the entire playbook. If you’re stuck in a high-interest auto note, copy the math and start tonight. The rumble in your ears is cool; the rumble of compound interest is louder.
