5 Money Habits That Keep You Broke (And What to Do Instead)

Break these common money habits holding you back. Practical fixes for impulsive spending, poor tracking, and other financial pitfalls.

Habit #1: “I’ll Budget Next Month” Mentality#

More than 60% of Americans don’t track spending consistently. This leads to:

  • Overspending on non-essentials
  • Missed bill payments
  • No emergency savings

Fix: Create a Maintenance Budget#

Set aside 15 minutes weekly to:

  1. Review major purchases
  2. Adjust category limits
  3. Update savings goals

Use our automated budget tracker to make updates painless.

Habit #2: Impulse Buying “Treats”#

Those $50-200 purchases add up:

  • Weekly lunch out: $100/month
  • Spontaneous Amazon buys: $150/month
  • Unused subscriptions: $50/month

Fix: The 30-Day Rule#

  1. Add desired items to a “Maybe” list
  2. Wait 30 days
  3. If still wanted, purchase

Track these in our spending journal template with a “Pending” column.

Habit #3: Mixing Checking Accounts#

Using one account for everything causes:

  • Overdraft fees
  • Poor spending visibility
  • Difficulty tracking goals

Fix: Implement the Envelope System#

Create virtual accounts:

  1. Main Checking
  2. Bills & Essentials
  3. Fun Money
  4. Savings Vaults

Our multi-account budget template automates transfers between these.

Habit #4: Ignoring Small Debts#

“I’ll pay it off eventually” thinking costs thousands:

DebtBalanceAPRMonthly PaymentYears to PayoffTotal Interest
Credit Card$3,00018%$606.5$2,230

Fix: Debt Avalanche Method#

  1. List debts from highest to lowest interest
  2. Make minimum payments on all
  3. Attack highest-rate debt first

Use our debt payoff calculator to create a custom plan.

Habit #5: DIY Financial Planning#

Spreadsheets that are:

  • Too complex to maintain
  • Missing key formulas
  • Not connected to goals

Fix: Use Purpose-Built Tools#

Our financial template bundle includes:

  • Debt trackers with payoff calculators
  • Net worth dashboards
  • Goal-based savings planners

Bottom Line#

Breaking these habits takes time but pays huge dividends. Start with small changes:

  1. Pick 1 habit to fix this month
  2. Implement the fix using our templates
  3. Review progress in 30 days

Your future self will thank you for making these shifts today.
