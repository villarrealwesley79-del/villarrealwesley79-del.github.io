Paying the **minimum** amount on your credit-card statement feels painless.  
A few taps and you’re done for the month.  
But that convenience comes at an eye-watering price.

Below we break down **exactly** how much extra money you hand over when you stick to the minimum—and how long you stay in debt.

---

## A Quick Example

Imagine you owe **$5,000** on a card with:

* 20.99 % APR  
* 2 % minimum-payment requirement (of the outstanding balance)

Here’s what happens if you pay only the minimum each month.

| Item                  | Value        |
|-----------------------|--------------|
| Initial balance       | $5,000       |
| Monthly payment start | $100         |
| **Total interest paid** | **$6,109**   |
| Time to payoff        | 20.6 years   |

You end up paying **$11,109** for a $5,000 purchase—and it takes **two decades** to finish.

---

## Why Minimum Payments Are So Expensive

1. **Interest compounds daily.**  
   Every day you carry a balance, the issuer adds interest on yesterday’s interest.

2. **Payments shrink as the balance drops.**  
   A 2 % minimum payment on $5,000 is $100, but on $500 it’s only $10.  
   That means less money goes toward principal, stretching repayment.

3. **Fees extend the timeline.**  
   Late-payment fees, cash-advance fees, and annual fees all get added to the balance and also accrue interest.

---

## How to Escape the Trap

| Strategy             | Effect on 20.99 % APR $5,000 balance |
|----------------------|---------------------------------------|
| Pay $100 **flat** monthly | Payoff in 8.9 years, $5,553 interest |
| Pay $150 monthly     | Payoff in 4.9 years, $2,352 interest |
| Pay $250 monthly     | Payoff in 2.3 years, $1,219 interest |
| 0 % balance-transfer card (18-month promo, $250/mo) | Paid off in 20 months, $0 interest |

Even a **fixed** $100 beats the variable minimum.  
Anything above that accelerates freedom.

---

## Key Takeaway

Minimum payments maximize **lender profits**, not your financial health.  
Set a **fixed** monthly amount—ideally well above the minimum—and automate it.  
Your future self will thank you.

---

### Quick Actions You Can Take Today

* Log in to your card issuer and switch to **autopay for a fixed amount**.  
* Use a debt-payoff calculator to pick a target date and required monthly payment.  
* If your credit is good, apply for a 0 % balance-transfer card and eliminate interest during the promo period.
