## How It Works

1. **Create income sources** for each side hustle or platform so totals roll up correctly.
2. **Log each payout** with date, gross amount, and hours worked if applicable.
3. **Record fees or adjustments** as separate lines to see net income by platform.
4. **Review monthly totals** to identify seasonality and trends in your earnings.
5. **Compare effective hourly rates** to focus on the gigs that pay best.

## What Makes This Different

Gig apps show earnings inside their own dashboard, but they do not give you a full picture across platforms. A spreadsheet brings every source together so you can see total income, compare hourly rates, and make smarter decisions about where to spend your time.

Apps also tend to focus on the gross payout without highlighting fees, bonuses, or changes over time. With a spreadsheet you can track the actual net number you take home and spot when a platform starts paying less. You can also separate tips and bonuses so you know what is predictable and what is not.

Because it is a spreadsheet, you control the data forever. If you stop driving, switch platforms, or add a new side hustle, your tracking system stays intact and you can compare year-over-year results without a subscription.

## Real-World Example

Say you drive for two platforms and sell products on Etsy. In February you earn $860 from Platform A across 42 hours, $540 from Platform B across 28 hours, and $320 from Etsy. You also pay $95 in platform fees and processing charges. The tracker shows $1,720 gross, $1,625 net, and effective hourly rates of $20.48 and $19.29 for the two driving platforms. That makes it clear your Etsy sales, while smaller, require less time and could be worth growing. You can use that insight to adjust your schedule next month.
