## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What's the difference between snowball and avalanche?**
A: Snowball pays smallest debts first for quick wins. Avalanche pays highest interest first to minimize total interest. This template shows both so you can choose.

**Q: How many debts can I track?**
A: The template supports up to 15 different debts with individual balances, interest rates, and minimum payments.

**Q: Can I see how extra payments help?**
A: Yes. Enter different extra payment amounts and instantly see how your payoff date and total interest change.

**Q: Does it work for student loans?**
A: Yes. The calculator works for any type of debt: credit cards, student loans, car loans, personal loans, medical debt, or mortgages.

**Q: Can I include promotional 0% APR balances?**
A: Yes. Enter the current balance and 0% rate so the schedule still tracks the payoff timeline.

**Q: What if my minimum payments change over time?**
A: Update the minimum payment cell for that debt and the schedule will recalculate from that point forward.

**Q: Does the template support biweekly or irregular extra payments?**
A: Yes. Add your average monthly extra payment and adjust it anytime to reflect changes in cash flow.

## How It Works

1. **List every debt** with balance, interest rate, and minimum payment. Each line becomes a trackable payoff target.
2. **Choose a payoff method** by selecting snowball or avalanche. The sheet shows both so you can compare the results.
3. **Set your extra payment amount** to see how additional cash accelerates the payoff date.
4. **Review the monthly schedule** for a clear, month-by-month plan and the exact date each debt disappears.
5. **Update when things change** like a rate adjustment or a balance transfer, and the entire plan recalculates.

## What Makes This Different

Debt payoff apps often lock the math behind a subscription and display a single timeline without showing the underlying calculation. A spreadsheet makes every number visible and editable. You can see the interest, the principal, and the exact payment order instead of trusting a black box.

Apps also tend to assume a perfect monthly routine. Real life is uneven. Some months you add a bonus payment, other months you just make minimums. With this spreadsheet you can change the extra payment amount at any time, compare scenarios, and keep control of the plan without paying for a premium tier.

Finally, spreadsheets are portable and permanent. You can save your plan, duplicate it, and experiment without worrying about account limits or price changes. When you reach zero, you still have the full history and can reuse the file for the next goal.

## Real-World Example

Suppose you have four debts: a $4,200 credit card at 21% with a $120 minimum, a $6,800 card at 18% with a $170 minimum, a $7,500 car loan at 6% with a $240 minimum, and a $1,500 medical bill at 0% with a $75 minimum. Your total minimums are $605 and you can add $300 extra each month, for $905 total. With the avalanche method, the spreadsheet directs the extra $300 to the 21% card first, then rolls that payment to the 18% card. The payoff timeline shows the first card gone in 6 months, the second in 14 months, the medical bill in 17 months, and the car loan in 32 months, saving hundreds in interest versus paying minimums only. If you prefer quick wins, switch to snowball and the schedule updates instantly so you can compare the tradeoff between speed and interest savings.
